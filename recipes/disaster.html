Recipe for Disaster

Home

Description

Servings

Way more than intended

Prep time

Alarmingly short

Cook time

Instant explosion or slow-burn meltdown

Serving Suggestion

Serve cold, with regret on the side. Usually followed by cleanup and hard lessons.

Ingredients

  • 1 overflowing cup of poor communication
  • 3 tablespoons of unchecked ego
  • 1/2 cup of rushed decisions
  • A generous drizzle of assumptions
  • 1 stick of bad timing
  • A handful of ignored red flags
  • 2 teaspoons of denial
  • A pinch (or fistful) of chaotic energy
  • Optional: overconfidence, mercury in retrograde

Steps

  1. Preheat environment to high tension. The hotter, the better.
  2. Mix poor communication and ego in a large bowl of misunderstanding. Stir until things start getting personal.
  3. Add rushed decisions and assumptions all at once—don’t bother checking the measurements.
  4. Toss in bad timing and ignored red flags. Do not sift.
  5. Season with denial. Trust us: the more you add, the more dramatic the final result.
  6. Bake until relationships are crispy and plans are scorched. Timing will vary depending on emotional volatility.
