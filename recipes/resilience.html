Recipe for Resilience

Home

Description

Servings

Infinite

Prep time

A lifetime

Cook time

Variable (some pressure required)

Serving Suggestion

Best served warm, alongside cup of perspective and some rest. Reheats well after setbacks.

Ingredients

  • 1 heaping cup of self-compassion
  • 2 tablespoons of grit
  • 1/2 cup of adaptability
  • A dash of humor
  • 3 generous spoonfuls of perspective
  • 1 teaspoon of vulnerability
  • 1 pinch of stubborn hope
  • A handful of support from others
  • Optional: tears, coffee, or loud music (use as needed)

Steps

  1. Start with self-compassion. This forms your base. Stir gently but thoroughly—no room for harsh self-talk here.
  2. Fold in grit and adaptability. These are your structure—what helps you bend without breaking.
  3. Sprinkle in humor. Even a little can change the flavor of the toughest moments.
  4. Add perspective slowly. Best when simmered over time. Helps you see beyond the moment.
  5. Whisk in vulnerability. It might feel counterintuitive, but it binds the rest together.
  6. Mix in stubborn hope. Even a small amount can go a long way. Let it rise, even when it doesn’t feel like it will.
  7. Garnish with support. Friends, family, pets, therapists—whatever works. Don't skimp on this step.
  8. Let it sit. Resilience builds over time. Don’t rush it.
